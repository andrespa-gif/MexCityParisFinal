Data appendix

Sources, proxies, and readings.

This page explains where the data came from, which variables are direct observations, which are proxies or reconstructions, and which readings shaped the interpretation.

The UQI is a historical visualization tool, not an official government index. It combines documented data, reconstructed historical series, and transparent proxies to compare alcaldías over time.

Core datasets

Where The Data Came From

INEGI Census and Marco Geoestadístico

INEGI census data supports population, inhabited dwellings, and modern alcaldía geography. The alcaldía boundary layer follows the present-day 16-borough structure so every decade can be compared with the same geography.

PNUD Human Development Index

HDI / IDH values come from the workbook’s PNUD municipal HDI sheet. HDI combines health, education, and income. The available comparison years are 1990, 2000, 2010, 2015, and 2020.

SEMOVI and Mexico City Mobility Sources

Transit access is based on station-equivalent access by alcaldía, with modes weighted by service quality and capacity. Metro is used as the benchmark mode.

SEDEMA Green Areas Inventory

Green space per capita uses current SEDEMA green-space totals divided by historical population. This makes the variable useful for showing pressure on available green space as population grew.

Hospitals, Museums, and Libraries

Hospital access counts major hospital-level institutions. Third spaces count museums and public libraries with documented opening dates, divided by population.

Proxy logic

How The UQI Variables Were Modeled

Transit AccessWeighted station-equivalents per 100,000 residents. Metro receives the benchmark weight because of its capacity and dedicated right of way.
ElectricityHistorical coverage by inhabited dwellings. By 2020 it is nearly universal, so the inequality is mostly historical.
Piped WaterCoverage of inhabited dwellings with piped water. This remains one of the clearest present-day infrastructure inequalities.
DrainageCoverage of inhabited dwellings with drainage. Rural and peripheral alcaldías reached near-universal coverage later.
Hospital AccessMajor hospitals per 100,000 residents. Small clinics and centros de salud are excluded to keep the measure consistent.
Green SpaceCurrent green-space totals divided by historical population. This is a pressure proxy, not a claim that historical park area was unchanged.
Third SpacesMuseums and public libraries per 100,000 residents. Used as a proxy for civic/cultural public infrastructure.
Excluded VariablesPopulation, density, persons per dwelling, and inhabited dwellings were removed from the UQI so the index focuses on urban services and amenities rather than demographic size or crowding.

Readings used

Knowledge Base and Interpretive Sources

Spatial Equity in Access to Urban Parks via Public Transit

Durán-Pérez, Núñez, and Gómez Gámez, Land 2025. This study shaped the park-access and centrality framing. It explains reach, gravity, and closeness as ways to think about how public transit connects residents to green space.

2025 STA Spotlight: Integrating Mobility in Mexico City

This reading shaped the mobility context: Integrated Mobility Card, Metro, Metrobús, trolleybus, RTP, Cablebús, Ecobici, and the attempt to target dense and marginalized areas.

Mexico City and the French / Paris Model

The historical framing comes from the timeline research: Paseo de la Reforma, Porfirian modernization, elite planned neighborhoods, and the contrast with colonias populares and peripheral infrastructure gaps.

Why HDI Is Separate From UQI

HDI is not part of the default UQI. It is used as a comparison outcome because it reflects human development, while UQI is treated as an urban-planning proxy based on services and amenities.

Limitations

What To Be Careful About

Several early historical values are reconstructed or back-cast. They are useful for narrative visualization but should not be treated like direct census observations. The UQI is also sensitive to variable selection: changing the selected variables changes the meaning of “urban quality.” The scatterplot shows association between UQI and HDI, not proof of causation.
