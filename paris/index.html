Paris, 1870-2020

The model city: Paris as planning reference.

Paris is the original Haussmannized capital that Mexico City imitated. This page applies the same UQI and HDI instrument to all 20 arrondissements.

Paris built the boulevard-and-sewer grammar that Porfirian elites copied. But even Paris distributes parks, transit, and amenities unevenly across arrondissements. Compare both cities on the parallel page.

Why Paris matters here

The city Mexico City tried to become

Paris under Haussmann (1853-1870) established the modern planning toolkit: wide boulevards, unified sewer and water networks, formal parks, and standardized building codes. Porfirian Mexico City imported that visual and technical language through Reforma, new parks, and elite neighborhoods.

Parallel question

Did Paris achieve equal urban quality across its arrondissements, or does its own spatial inequality mirror what Mexico City inherited?

Paris scores higher overall on infrastructure coverage, but the UQI map still shows variation: western and central arrondissements retain more green space and transit access per capita, while eastern arrondissements carry higher density and lower amenity scores.

What this shows

In Paris, UQI and HDI align strongly: arrondissements with more transit, green space, and civic amenities also rank higher on health, education, and income. The 7e, 8e, and 16e lead on both measures; the 18e, 19e, and 20e lag.
