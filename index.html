What both cities show

Both maps show spatial concentration of planning quality. Infrastructure, parks, transit, and civic amenities cluster in central and western districts. HDI follows the same gradient: neighborhoods with stronger UQI scores also rank higher on health, education, and income.

How the cities differ

Paris reached near-universal water, drainage, and electricity coverage by the early 20th century, so its UQI lines converge more tightly. Mexico City shows wider spread: peripheral alcaldías improved later and remain lower. Paris is the model; Mexico City is the uneven copy — selective Haussmannization without equal peripheral investment.

The stakes are lived inequality: where you are born in either city still shapes access to parks, hospitals, transit, and the conditions that determine how long and how well you live.
